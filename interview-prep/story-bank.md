# Story Bank — Master STAR+R Stories

This file accumulates your best interview stories over time. Each evaluation (Block F) adds new stories here. Instead of memorizing 100 answers, maintain 5-10 deep stories that you can bend to answer almost any behavioral question.

## How it works

1. Every time `/career-ops oferta` generates Block F (Interview Plan), new STAR+R stories get appended here
2. Before your next interview, review this file — your stories are already organized by theme
3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine 2-3 stories into a narrative
   - "Tell me about your most impactful project" → pick your highest-impact story
   - "Tell me about a conflict you resolved" → find a story with a Reflection

## Stories

### [Network/Closing] The 40-Deal Rolodex
**Source:** Reports #001 Coinbase, #002 BitGo, #003 Kraken — Listings/Ecosystem BD roles
**S (Situation):** Bobos of War was one NFT project among thousands competing for attention in the saturated 2021–22 market.
**T (Task):** Build a partnership engine from zero — no brand, no warm list, no budget for paid acquisition.
**A (Action):** Ran a systematic inbound + outbound BD motion across Discord, Twitter, and direct founder DMs: qualified targets, pitched collaborations, negotiated terms, closed.
**R (Result):** 40+ partnerships closed with crypto and NFT projects; direct relationships with founders, core teams, and community leads that persist today.
**Reflection:** Volume came from systematizing outreach, not charisma. Next time I'd CRM-track the pipeline from day one — the data would have made prioritization even sharper.
**Best for questions about:** track record of exceeding goals, sourcing/closing, "why should we trust your network," outbound strategy

### [Community/GTM] The $2M Community-Led Raise
**Source:** Report #001 — Coinbase — BD Associate, Listings (Base Builders)
**S (Situation):** Launching an NFT project with no institutional backing in a market where most launches failed.
**T (Task):** Fund the project and build a durable community simultaneously.
**A (Action):** Co-founded and executed a community-led go-to-market: owned the full funnel from lead-gen to close, built the community on Discord/Twitter as the distribution engine.
**R (Result):** Raised over $2M; the community became the project's moat and the source of inbound partnership demand.
**Reflection:** Community isn't marketing — it's the product's distribution layer. The same playbook applies to representing a listings platform to an ecosystem of builders.
**Best for questions about:** most impactful project, representing a brand to a community, GTM, "tell me about yourself" anchor

### [Cross-functional Delivery] Pitch-to-Go-Live Engine (Bobo Olympics)
**Source:** Reports #002 BitGo, #004 Extsy
**S (Situation):** Weekly cross-project events, each requiring negotiated terms, creative assets, and launch timelines across four functions (art, smart-contract dev, marketing, community ops).
**T (Task):** Move every deal from handshake to delivered event without missed launches — repeatedly, on a calendar.
**A (Action):** Pitched and negotiated each event as a deal, then coordinated cross-team delivery across timezones with clear owners and async-first documentation.
**R (Result):** A multi-week flagship series (Bobo Olympics) plus a sustained weekly cadence delivered for years — every event a closed, executed deal.
**Reflection:** A written runbook per deal type cut coordination cost massively. The same outreach→terms→go-live motion is exactly how a listing moves from application to launch.
**Best for questions about:** cross-functional coordination, project delivery, working with eng/product/compliance, repeatable process ownership

### [Markets/Judgment] Treasury Through the Cycle
**Source:** Report #002 — BitGo — Associate, Listings & Ecosystem Development
**S (Situation):** Project treasury exposed to violently volatile token and NFT markets, 2020–2024.
**T (Task):** Preserve runway and fund operations through a full market cycle.
**A (Action):** Owned treasury and budget allocation; made position and spend decisions from on-chain data and crypto market-structure reads rather than sentiment.
**R (Result):** The project operated for four years across an entire boom-bust cycle without running out of runway.
**Reflection:** Position sizing and a written rationale beat gut feel in volatile markets. This is the "data-driven, translate trends into strategy" muscle institutional crypto teams ask for.
**Best for questions about:** analytical decision-making, judgment under ambiguity, crypto market knowledge, handling downturns

### [Operations/Rigor] Two Salons, Full Stack
**Source:** Reports #002 BitGo, #004 Extsy
**S (Situation):** Co-owner of two nail salon locations with 11 employees and real regulatory stakes.
**T (Task):** Run the entire back office solo while growing the business.
**A (Action):** Owned licensing, compliance, payroll, bookkeeping, vendor contracts, and financial reporting for six years; negotiated vendor terms and managed inventory across both locations.
**R (Result):** Six years of profitable, compliant operation; revenue growth via pricing, service expansion, and retention programs.
**Reflection:** Unglamorous ops discipline is a moat in BD orgs — closers who also handle contracting, billing, and admin cleanly are rare. Detail work under real stakes transfers everywhere.
**Best for questions about:** attention to detail, contracting/admin/billing duties, P&L ownership, account management, process and audit discipline

### [Resilience/Bear Market] Bear-Market BD
**Source:** Report #003 — Kraken — Sr Associate, BD (Asset Listing)
**S (Situation):** The 2022–23 crypto downturn killed most NFT projects' partnership pipelines along with their treasuries.
**T (Task):** Keep generating new partnerships when nobody was buying and attention had evaporated.
**A (Action):** Shifted pitch framing from hype to mutual survival value (shared audiences, cost-split events), leaned harder on the warm founder network, kept the weekly event cadence alive as proof of life.
**R (Result):** Continued closing partnerships through the bear; the project outlived most of its 2021 cohort.
**Reflection:** "Generating new business regardless of market conditions" is mostly about repricing your value proposition fast when the market regime changes.
**Best for questions about:** resilience, selling in downturns, adaptability, "tell me about a time things went wrong"

### [Empathy/Positioning] Issuer-Side Empathy
**Source:** Reports #001 Coinbase, #003 Kraken
**S (Situation):** For four years Viet sat on the issuer/founder side of platform and partnership conversations — the side applying for visibility, listings, and collaborations.
**T (Task):** Get a no-name token/NFT project taken seriously by established platforms and partners.
**A (Action):** Prepared tokenomics documentation, community metrics, and due-diligence answers; learned exactly which signals make platform teams say yes.
**R (Result):** $2M+ raised and 40+ partnerships with established ecosystem players.
**Reflection:** Having been the asset issuer is the empathy advantage for exchange-side listings work: I know the issuer's anxieties, timelines, and unspoken questions because they were mine. It converts "zero exchange-side experience" from a gap into an angle.
**Best for questions about:** "you've never worked at an exchange," understanding clients, due diligence, why listings BD

### [Pressure/Service] Chef-Speed Under Pressure
**Source:** Report #002 — BitGo (personal attributes: high energy, fast-paced environment)
**S (Situation):** Started as a Line Cook at Driftwood Kitchen (DC) in a high-volume kitchen.
**T (Task):** Master every station and earn the kitchen's trust at peak service speed.
**A (Action):** Built prep systems, held standards under rush pressure, escalated calmly; earlier at Paku Pakus, combined kitchen and front-of-house service.
**R (Result):** Promoted Line Cook → Executive Chef in 10 months; at Paku Pakus, highest average check ($19) and tip percentage (26%) on staff.
**Reflection:** High-pressure service work is the best BD training nobody counts: read the room fast, deliver under load, never let the customer feel the chaos.
**Best for questions about:** fast-paced environments, work ethic, customer-facing instincts, career-change narrative
