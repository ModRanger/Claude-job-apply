### Gaps & Mitigation

1. **No formal EA title / never supported a C-level or VP executive (HARD GAP).** Mozilla's wording allows "multi location environment," which the salons technically satisfy, but the clause "working on administrative tasks for 1 or more executives" is the recruiter's screen-out filter. Viet did this work for himself as an owner, not for an executive. Adjacent evidence exists (scheduling, expenses-equivalent bookkeeping, travel logistics for events) but a recruiter scanning for "EA to VP/C-suite, 5 yrs" will not find it. Mitigation: cover letter line — "I ran the calendar, payroll, books, licensing and vendor relationships for an 11-person, two-location business for six years — the same portfolio an EA owns, with my own capital at stake." Honest assessment: mitigation lowers but does not remove screen-out risk, which is HIGH at this comp band (the $95–151K range will attract deep pools of career EAs from FAANG-adjacent companies).
2. **No corporate tooling evidence (Google Workspace, expense systems).** Nice-to-have gap. Mitigation: add a tools line to CV (Google Workspace, Sheets, Slack — only if true).
3. **No experience inside a large (500+) organization.** Mozilla wants someone who can navigate org politics across Communications/Marketing/Policy. Viet has only worked in <15-person structures. Mitigation: frame the 40+ partner coordination as multi-stakeholder navigation; acknowledge learning curve.

## C) Level & Strategy

- **Level detected:** Mid-senior corporate EA (5+ yrs formal). **Viet's natural level for this archetype:** entry-to-mid — transferable skills are real but unproven in a corporate support seat.
- **Overqualification / flight-risk narrative (founder applying to EA role):** A hiring manager will ask "why does a co-founder who raised $2M want to manage my calendar?" Credible answer: "I've spent ten years being the principal — carrying the P&L, the payroll, the risk. What I learned is that I'm exceptional at the operating machinery behind a leader: scheduling, logistics, records, vendor management, follow-through. I want to apply that machinery in service of executives at a mission-driven company without carrying founder risk, and I'm deliberate about that trade." This narrative is workable but stronger at startups than at Mozilla, where the EA craft bar (board cycles, exec comms polish) is itself senior.
- **Sell-without-lying plan:** lead with the salon ops portfolio (scheduling/payroll/compliance/bookkeeping for 11 staff, 2 locations, 6 years — cv.md L26-29), then async distributed coordination (L19), then discretion/treasury (L20). Never claim "EA experience"; claim "owner-operator who did the EA function for his own business."
- **If downleveled:** there is no lower EA req open at Mozilla; downleveling isn't available here.

## Recommendation

**SKIP (2.8/5).** The role itself is excellent (top-quartile comp, fully remote, stable org), but the formal bar — 5+ years of administrative support *for executives* — is a hard gap, and at $95–151K the applicant pool will be stacked with career tech EAs. Screen-out probability is high; the application would mostly cost recruiter time and Viet's time. If Viet feels strongly about Mozilla, the better play is a referral/DM to a Mozilla EA or the recruiting team with the owner-operator framing before applying. Comp floor check: passes ($95K+ ≥ $45K) — not a comp skip, a fit skip.
